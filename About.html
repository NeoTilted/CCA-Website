About the Corps

The National Cadet Corps (Air) in Singapore, established in 1969, is a uniformed youth organization supported by the Ministry of Education that aims to develop responsible and resilient leaders through military-related activities. Originally focused on aviation and basic military skills, it has evolved to offer comprehensive training aligned with the Republic of Singapore Air Force's standards. Cadets undergo drills, fieldcraft, and aviation knowledge training, with opportunities for flight experiences and visits to the RSAF. Emphasizing character building, leadership, and teamwork, NCC Air prepares cadets to serve the nation and excel in their future endeavors.

Mission & Vision

"Our Mission is to nurture inspiring leaders and committed citizens through adventurous, challenging, and military-inspired experiences, while upholding a Vision to forge extraordinary youth leaders who lead with honor, courage, and excellence."

NCC Air Roles and Responsibilities

Role Assistant Role Description
T'O (Teacher Officer) - Teachers serving as official officers overseeing NCC Air activities.
MSG (Master Sergeant) ASM (Assistant Sergeant Major) Highest-ranking cadet responsible for leading and managing the entire company; ASM assists MSG in command and organization.
PS A & PS B (Platoon Sergeants) APS (Assistant Platoon Sergeants) Lead respective platoons and ensure discipline and training; APS supports platoon management and welfare.
Aero-I (Aeromodelling Instructor) AAero-I (Assistant Aeromodelling Instructor) Oversees aeromodelling training and projects; assistant supports activities and instruction.
DSI (Drill Specialist Instructor) ADSI (Assistant Drill Specialist Instructor) Conducts precision drill training; assistant aids in drills and coordination.
PTI (Physical Training Instructor) APTI (Assistant Physical Training Instructor) Responsible for cadets' physical fitness and conditioning; assistant helps organize fitness sessions.
CWO (Cadet Welfare Officer) ACWO (Assistant Cadet Welfare Officer) Ensures cadet health and welfare, including medical needs; assistant supports medical and welfare tasks.
QM (Quartermaster) AQM (Assistant Quartermaster) Manages equipment, uniforms, and logistics; assistant helps maintain inventory and logistics.
Admin AAdmin (Assistant Admin) Handles administrative tasks such as attendance and records; assistant helps track cadet information.
