THE AIR JOURNEY

These are activities hosted in NYP

Drills

Cadets begin their journey with basic drill commands, focusing on synchronization and unity as a team. As they progress and master these fundamentals, they move on to advanced drill movements and develop a strong sense of discipline, teamwork, and kinship.

Precision Drill Squad (PDS)

PDS is a showcase of discipline, coordination, and flair. Cadets perform advanced rifle drills with sharp precision and rhythmic execution. Through dedicated training, they master complex routines that build confidence, teamwork, and a deep sense of pride in their performance.

Physical Training

PT builds the strength, endurance, and resilience essential for every cadet. Through regular exercises, fitness circuits, and team-based challenges, cadets develop not only physical fitness but also mental toughness and determination—forming the foundation for all other NCC activities.

Aero-Modelling

Aeromodelling introduces cadets to the fundamentals of flight and aircraft design. Through hands-on activities like building and flying model aircraft, cadets gain insights into aerodynamics, engineering concepts, and aviation skills—sparking curiosity and a passion for aerospace.

Map Navigation

Map Navigation equips cadets with essential orienteering skills. Cadets learn to read topographic maps, use a compass, and navigate unfamiliar terrain with confidence. These skills promote critical thinking, independence, and situational awareness during field exercises.

Free Style Drill (FSD)

FSD trains cadets in fire prevention, emergency response, and basic rescue techniques. Through practical drills and simulations, cadets learn to handle fire safety equipment and respond calmly in emergencies—developing readiness and a strong sense of civic duty.

Annual Camp

The Annual Camp is the highlight of every cadet’s journey. Over several days, cadets participate in a range of challenging and exciting activities such as fieldcraft, team-building games, and leadership workshops. It’s a chance to forge new friendships, grow in confidence, and experience life as a cohesive unit beyond the classroom.

Community Service

Community service is a core part of the NCC experience, instilling a sense of responsibility and compassion. Cadets take part in meaningful outreach efforts—such as environmental clean-ups, charity drives, and support for the elderly—building empathy, civic-mindedness, and a strong spirit of giving back.

Senior Specialist Leadership Course

The Senior Specialist Leadership Course (SSLC) trains cadets in leadership, instruction, and planning. Cadets learn how to guide their peers and conduct unit activities effectively. This course prepares them to take on key leadership roles within their unit.

Introduction to NCC Air

NCC Camp Forge

Camp Forge is a field trip that Year One cadets must attend. For many, this is their first visit to NCC HQ. Cadets will participate in fun and thrilling activities such as archery, paintball shooting, navigating the Motivational Obstacle Course, and more—organised by NCC HQ.

NCC Camp Steel

Camp Steel is a Field Trip that Cadets will attend in Year Two. Cadets will participate in fun events such as high element with a zip gliding at the end, learning how to disassemble and re-assemble a real SAR-21 and much more that is organized by NCC HQ.
